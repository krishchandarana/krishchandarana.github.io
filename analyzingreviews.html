Product Review Analyzer

A way for customers to ensure the purchase the right product online.

Why I Built A Review Analyzer?

Online shopping is become more and more popular each day.

With online shopping become more popular, people will be dependant on one main factor when purchasing products online, which is reviews. Reviews of a product are what people depend on because they are not able to see the product first hand, therefore it leads them to depend on the opinions of people that actually have the product.

After a survey conducted on twitter on which 100+ people voted, many people strongly depend on reivews. The problem is that when a customer is looking at reviews, many times you might miss out on valuable reivews and only see the fake reviews. A solution needs to be made in order to ensure that a customer is able to understand all reviews without comprimising on time.

Check below to learn more about how I helped customers understand reviews without comprimising on time!

How I Built A Review Analyzer?

After realizing the problem, I decided to build an algorithim to analyze reviews for a product, and provide the customer with an understanding of the most common words in reviews, for both positive comments and negative comments.

I used Natural Language Proccessing (NLP) in order to perform text analysis on the reviews, and the program was built using python. The first step for the program is to look at the star rating of the review and detect the number of stars, and if the program detects 2 or less stars then the review will be classified as negative, however if the program detects 4 or more stars then the review will be classified as positive. In most reviews the number of stars the rating is out of is 5. Once the program has seperated the negative and positive reivews, it performs text analysis, and it captures the most common postive and negative words to describe the product and outputs the results in a word map for the customer to see. There are two word maps for the customer to see, one for negative and the other for positive, and the bigger the word on the word map, the more times it was mentioned. This gives the customer a basic understanding of all the positive and negative words used to describe the product.

Making this model was such an awesome project, and I had a great time doing it. The end result was super cool, however the best part of implementing the model was actually programming it. Hopefully, this algorithim can help customers make the right desicion for them when purchasing a product online.
